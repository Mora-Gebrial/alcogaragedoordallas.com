How we install a garage door for you




  • Preparation

    Our skilled technicians begin with great care gathering all the necessary tools and materials for the installation process. We ensure that the workspace is clear and free from any obstructions to facilitate a smooth setup.


  • Frame Installation

    With precision and care we commence by installing the door frame components including the vertical and horizontal tracks and also the mounting brackets. Following the manufacturer's instructions meticulously we make certain that the track is perfectly level and firmly fixed to the wall.


  • Panel Installation

    Every door panel is meticulously attached to the frame, starting from the bottom and progressing upwards. We carefully install each panel and make sure it is aligned correctly and has consistent spacing for maximum functionality by using the equipment that is supplied.


  • Spring Installation

    Our proficient techs handle the installation of torsion or extension springs with the utmost caution and expertise. Understanding the potential hazards associated with high tension springs, we adhere strictly to manufacturer guidelines to ensure safe and effective setting up.


  • Track Alignment

    We meticulously inspect and adjust the alignment of the tracks to guarantee smooth and efficient door operation. Utilizing precision tools, we verify that the tracks are plumb and parallel, making any necessary adjustments to achieve optimal alignment.


  • Hardware Installation

    Our technicians expertly attach rollers, hinges, plus other necessary equipment to the door panels, following manufacturer specifications to the letter. We meticulously ensure that all hardware is securely fastened and properly aligned to ensure seamless functioning.


  • Final Adjustments and Testing

    Before concluding the installation process make sure that the garage gate operates well so we test it thoroughly. Our technicians carefully check for any potential issues, making precise adjustments to spring tension, and track alignment and hardware as needed to ensure optimal performance.
